In the marvellous city of Rio de Janeiro, almost one-quarter of the people live in Favelas. Those informal settlements have been subject to more than a century of government neglect, so basic services like water and sewage systems and waste collection have to be organised by themselves. Favelas also have some urbanistic qualities that are often ignored, like low cost, centrally located housing, pedestrian-oriented streets, tight-knit community life and unrestrained entrepreneurial and cultural activities.

The Sustainable Favela Network, facilitated by the NGO Catalytic Communities, was created to strengthen community programs promoting sustainability and resilience in Rio's Favelas. We bring together community leaders and waste collectors that organise dugnads and purposeful actions to support community initiatives that encourage solid waste solutions.

Last year the Climate Act Club and ISS community have successfully fundraised 8000 NOK which were used to buy food for 50 Recyclable Collectors families who could not work due to Covid.

The objective of the Sustainable Favela Network this year is to promote Climate Justice in the Favelas. We will do this by implementing practical solutions that demonstrate the sustainable potential of Favelas and by promoting tangible paths the other Favelas in Brazil and in the rest of the world can follow to become more sustainable communities.

The two infrastructure projects that we will implement this year is to build a green roof and install solar panels in community centres, located and run by Favela residents with the participation of over 200 community leaders that will be trained to replicate the projects in their local communities.

We will also make diverse knowledge and skills exchanges between Favela’s organizers including collective action and education events in person and online. The exchanges will cover various sustainable technologies like community clean up, training on recycling techniques, construction of composting piles and vegetable patches.
